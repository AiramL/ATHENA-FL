Building Federated Learning Accurate Models by Client Clustering

Federated learning is a new paradigm that enables the training of machine learning models through collaboration between clients and an aggregation server. Training eliminates the need to share private data, giving clients privacy of their samples. However, when clients have distinct data distributions, training presents convergence difficulties, resulting in predictive errors in the final model. This paper proposes a federated learning system that considers clients with heterogeneous data distributions and yet produces accurate models in fewer training epochs than the state of the art. The effects of data heterogeneity are mitigated by clustering clients based on an estimate of the data distribution through the weights of the locally trained neural network. In addition, the system uses the one-against-all technique, it trains one detector for each class in the system. Thus, different groups can combine the detectors to form a model that can detect classes from other groups. The results show that the one-against-all model has a high ability to correctly identify samples and up to 18% higher accuracy than traditional training, with a low communication cost during training, reducing the amount of transmitted bytes between 43% and 92% compared to the MobileNet architecture.
